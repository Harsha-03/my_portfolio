## How He Thinks About Design

Harsha approaches design by finding where the user experience actually breaks.

He does not start with visual polish. He starts with the user moment that creates confusion, delay, mistrust, or friction. From there, he works through the loop: understand the problem, map the flow, design the interaction, test the decision, ship, and iterate.

His design philosophy is simple:

Design does not ship perfect. It ships, gets used, reveals what is wrong, and improves.

That mindset shows up across his work. In Starbucks Mobile Order, he did not redesign the app to make it look better. He identified that the real issue was missing state visibility between order placement and pickup. In LifeOS, he did not build another productivity dashboard. He designed a system around recovery, reflection, and behavioral adaptation. In Resume Tailor, he did not just create an AI resume tool. He redesigned the experience around trust, honest scoring, and user control.

## Design Style

Harsha’s design style is clear, intentional, and product-focused.

He cares about:

* making user states visible
* reducing unnecessary cognitive load
* turning vague problems into structured flows
* designing for trust, recovery, and clarity
* creating interfaces that feel useful before they feel decorative
* testing assumptions through real feedback

He values design decisions that can be explained, defended, shipped, and improved.
